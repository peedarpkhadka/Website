Linear Algebra

What is Linear Algebra?

Linear Algebra studies vectors, matrices, and linear transformations, providing tools to solve systems of equations and analyze multidimensional data. It’s essential in computer graphics (3D rendering), machine learning (data transformations), and engineering (structural analysis).

Vectors and Matrices

  • Vectors: Ordered lists of numbers representing magnitude and direction. A 3D vector \( \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} \) might represent a point in space.
  • Matrices: Rectangular arrays of numbers. A 2x3 matrix like \( \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 & 3 \\ 4 & 5 & 6 \end{bmatrix} \) can encode relationships between variables.

Matrix Operations

  • Addition: Add corresponding elements of same-sized matrices. For \( \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 3 & 4 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 6 \\ 7 & 8 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 8 \\ 10 & 12 \end{bmatrix} \).
  • Scalar Multiplication: Multiply every element by a number. For \( 3 \cdot \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 3 & 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 6 \\ 9 & 12 \end{bmatrix} \).
  • Matrix Multiplication: Multiply rows of the first matrix by columns of the second. For \( \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 3 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \cdot \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 6 \\ 7 & 8 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 19 & 22 \\ 43 & 50 \end{bmatrix} \).

Solving Systems of Equations

A system like:

\[ \begin{cases} 3x + 2y = 7 \\ x - 4y = -5 \end{cases} \]

can be represented as:

\[ \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 2 \\ 1 & -4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 7 \\ -5 \end{bmatrix} \]

Gaussian Elimination Example

Augmented matrix: \( \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 2 & | & 7 \\ 1 & -4 & | & -5 \end{bmatrix} \).

Swap rows for simplicity: \( \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -4 & | & -5 \\ 3 & 2 & | & 7 \end{bmatrix} \).

Eliminate \( x \) from row 2: Subtract 3 times row 1 from row 2:

  • Row 2: \( (3 - 3 \cdot 1, 2 - 3 \cdot (-4), 7 - 3 \cdot (-5)) = (0, 14, 22) \).
  • New matrix: \( \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -4 & | & -5 \\ 0 & 14 & | & 22 \end{bmatrix} \).

Solve row 2: \( 14y = 22 \) → \( y = \frac{22}{14} = \frac{11}{7} \).

Back-substitute into row 1: \( x - 4 \left(\frac{11}{7}\right) = -5 \) → \( x - \frac{44}{7} = -5 \) → \( x = -5 + \frac{44}{7} = -\frac{35}{7} + \frac{44}{7} = \frac{9}{7} \).

Solution: \( x = \frac{9}{7} \), \( y = \frac{11}{7} \).

Advanced Topics

  • Determinants: Measure how a matrix scales space. For \( \begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{bmatrix} \), the determinant is \( ad - bc \).
  • Eigenvalues: Special scalars where \( A \mathbf{v} = \lambda \mathbf{v} \). These are vital in stability analysis and data compression.

Applications

  • Computer Graphics: Matrices transform 3D models for rendering.
  • Data Science: Principal Component Analysis (PCA) uses eigenvalues to reduce dimensionality.
