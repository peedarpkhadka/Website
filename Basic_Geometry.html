Basic Geometry

What is Geometry?

Geometry explores shapes, sizes, and spatial relationships. It’s practical in architecture (building design), navigation (GPS), and even game development (collision detection).

Fundamental Elements in Geometry

  • Points: Locations with no dimensions.
  • Lines: Infinite straight paths.
  • Angles: Measured in degrees; acute (< 90°), obtuse (> 90°), right (90°).

Shapes and Properties

  • Triangles: Sum of angles = 180°. Equilateral (all sides equal), isosceles (two sides equal), scalene (no sides equal).
  • Quadrilaterals: Rectangle (opposite sides equal, 90° angles), parallelogram (opposite sides parallel), trapezoid (one pair of parallel sides).
  • Circles: Radius \( r \), diameter \( 2r \), circumference \( 2\pi r \), area \( \pi r^2 \).

Example: Triangle Area and Pythagorean Theorem

Triangle with base 6, height 4, legs 3 and 4:

  • Area: \( \frac{1}{2} \cdot 6 \cdot 4 = 12 \).
  • Hypotenuse (right triangle): \( 3^2 + 4^2 = 9 + 16 = 25 \) → \( c = 5 \).
  • Perimeter: \( 3 + 4 + 5 = 12 \).

3D Geometry

  • Cube: Volume \( s^3 \), surface area \( 6s^2 \).
  • Sphere: Volume \( \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3 \), surface area \( 4\pi r^2 \).

Applications

  • Engineering: Geometry ensures structural integrity in bridges and buildings.
  • Art: Perspective drawing relies on geometric principles.
