Statistics and Probability

What is Statistics and Probability?

  • Statistics: Analyzes and interprets data to uncover patterns. It’s used in polls, scientific research, and business analytics.
  • Probability: Quantifies uncertainty, predicting the likelihood of events. It underpins statistical inference.

Measures of Central Tendency

  • Mean: Average of all values. For 2, 4, 6, 8, 10: \( \frac{2+4+6+8+10}{5} = 6 \).
  • Median: Middle value in ordered data. For 1, 3, 5, 7, 9, median is 5.
  • Mode: Most frequent value. For 1, 2, 2, 3, 4, mode is 2.

Measures of Dispersion

  • Range: Max minus min. For 1, 3, 5, 7, 9, range is \( 9 - 1 = 8 \).
  • Variance: Average squared deviation from the mean. For 1, 3, 5: Mean = 3, variance = \( \frac{(1-3)^2 + (3-3)^2 + (5-3)^2}{3} = \frac{4+0+4}{3} = \frac{8}{3} \).
  • Standard Deviation: Square root of variance, here \( \sqrt{\frac{8}{3}} \approx 1.63 \).

Example: Analyzing a Dataset

Dataset: 3, 5, 5, 7, 9, 11:

  • Mean: \( \frac{3+5+5+7+9+11}{6} = \frac{40}{6} \approx 6.67 \).
  • Median: Ordered: 3, 5, 5, 7, 9, 11 → average of 5 and 7 = 6.
  • Mode: 5 (appears twice).
  • Variance: \( \frac{(3-6.67)^2 + 2(5-6.67)^2 + (7-6.67)^2 + (9-6.67)^2 + (11-6.67)^2}{6} \approx 7.22 \).
  • Standard Deviation: \( \sqrt{7.22} \approx 2.69 \).

Probability Concepts

  • Basic Probability: \( P(\text{event}) = \frac{\text{favorable outcomes}}{\text{total outcomes}} \). Rolling a 4 on a die: \( \frac{1}{6} \).
  • Independent Events: \( P(A \text{ and } B) = P(A) \cdot P(B) \). Two coin flips both heads: \( \frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{1}{2} = \frac{1}{4} \).
  • Conditional Probability: \( P(A|B) = \frac{P(A \text{ and } B)}{P(B)} \). Example: Drawing two aces from a deck without replacement.

Example: Conditional Probability

From a deck of 52 cards, draw two cards without replacement. Probability the second is an ace given the first is an ace:

  • \( P(\text{first ace}) = \frac{4}{52} = \frac{1}{13} \).
  • After one ace, 51 cards remain, 3 aces: \( P(\text{second ace} | \text{first ace}) = \frac{3}{51} = \frac{1}{17} \).

Applications

  • Healthcare: Probability models disease spread; statistics evaluates treatment efficacy.
  • Finance: Risk assessment uses variance and probability distributions.
